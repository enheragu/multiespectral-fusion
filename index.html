Static early fusion merges raw pixel data before any processing. The visible and thermal images are combined at the channel level using fixed mathematical operations.

Early reprojection fusion applies a frequency or statistical decomposition to each channel before fusion. The transformed coefficients from RGB and thermal are combined per-band, then the result is projected back to a 3-channel image space.

Early adaptive α-fusion computes a per-pixel blending weight α from a local saliency measure (gradient, variance, or patch contrast). Each output channel is formed as α·RGB + (1−α)·T, letting the most informative source dominate at each location.
